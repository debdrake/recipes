Banan Cheesecake Squares

Desserts

Difficulty: Easy Source: cheneetoday.com

Ingredients

CRUST

1½ cup vanilla wafer crumbs

3 tablespoon granulated sugar

4 tablespoon salted butter - melted


FILLING

1½ medium overripe bananas - puréed

3 8 oz cream cheese softened

2 eggs

1 can sweetened condensed milk

1 teaspoon vanilla


CUSTARD TOPPING

8 oz milk

1 3oz pkg vanilla or French vanilla instant pudding

1-1/2 cups whipping cream

1/4 cup powdered sugar

1/2 tsp vanilla extract

4 oz sour cream

1/2 14oz can sweetened condensed milk


mini vanilla wafers

bananas

vanilla wafer crumbs

Directions

CRUST
Preheat oven to 350°F and line a 9x13 baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang to allow for easy removal.
Crush vanilla wafers with a food processor, or place in a gallon freezer bag and crush with a rolling pin. Pour into a medium bowl.
Add granulated sugar and stir to combine.
Add melted butter and stir well until mixture is well saturated with butter.
Pour into baking pan and press evenly into the bottom of the pan.
Bake in preheated oven for 10 minutes. Remove from oven and let cool.

FILLING
Reduce oven temperature to 325°F.
In a large bowl, purée overripe bananas, add cream cheese, 2 eggs, condensed milk and vanilla. Beet until smooth. Pour into pan and place on top of a water bath.

Bake in preheated oven for 30 minutes, or until outside of pan is puffed but center of cheesecake still jiggles slightly.
Cool completely at room temperature, then cover and chill at least 4 hours or overnight.

CUSTARD TOPPING
Whisk together milk with instant pudding in a large bowl until smooth. Let stand until very thick, 5 to 7 minutes.

Meanwhile, beat cream with an electric mixer on high speed until soft peaks form, 1 to 2 minutes. Gradually add powdered sugar; beat until stiff peaks form, 30 seconds to 1 minute. Stir in vanilla. Reserve 1 cup of the whipped cream in a small bowl; set aside.

Whisk sour cream and sweetened condensed milk into thickened pudding mixture until smooth and fluffy. Fold in remaining whipped cream until smooth and fluffy.

Top cheesecake layer with fresh banana slices and mini vanilla wafers. Pour custard on top, and cut into squares. Alternatively, you can cut the cheesecake squares first and top each with custard individually.

Sprinkle with more vanilla wafer crumbs, if desired. Serve immediately or refrigerate until ready to serve, keeping in mind these squares are best served the same day.
