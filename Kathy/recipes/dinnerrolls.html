Dinner Rolls

INGREDIENTS

  • - 2 c. warm water (warm, not hot) - 5-6 c. flour
  • 2 tablespoons yeast
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/3 c. sugar
  • 1/3 c. milk
  • 1 egg, slightly beaten
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons salt

METHOD

  1. 1. Put the warm water and a tsp of the sugar in a bowl. Stir in the yeast until it dissolves. Cover with plastic wrap then let sit for 10-12 minutes until yeast starts reacting If the yeast doesn't react, throw it out and start over.
  2. Make dough - add the rest of the sugar, the oil, salt, milk and egg, along with 2 cups of the flour. Beat until smooth.
  3. Add the next 2 to 2 1/2 cups of flour a half cup at a time. I use a stand mixer set on slow for a couple of minutes to mix, then turn it up to medium high. If the dough releases from the side of the bowl, then that is enough flour. If not, add 1/4 to a 1/2 cup more of flour, mix slow, then fast again. To finish kneading, continue to mix on med-high for 4-6 minutes. Turn out on a floured surface and roll in just enough dough to handle. It should be kinda sticky. Put it in a bowl*, and cover with plastic wrap and a towel. Let rise for 1 hour**.
    * I don’t oil the bowl. Dough expands better if it has something to hold onto. It also means I have to scrape the dough out of the bowl after rising.
    ** My best rise happens year around, in the microwave. Yes people get annoyed because they have to move it in and out to warm up their food. They don’t get rolls.
  4. Line two baking pans with parchment paper, split the dough in two, set half aside. Out of the 1/2 you are working with, form the dough into 12-18 equal-sized balls and lay on baking pan. Do other half of dough. Cover rolls with a clean dish towel, and let rise again for approximately an hour.
  5. A half hour into the rise, turn on the oven to 400 deg F. Bake for 8-12 minutes, until just golden. Rub cold butter on the tops of the warm rolls and serve.
