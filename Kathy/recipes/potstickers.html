Pot Stickers (gyoza)

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 ounces ground pork (don't use reduced fat pork)
  • 6 green onions, chopped fine
  • 3 - 6 cloves garlic, chopped fine
  • 1/2 head cabbage, chopped small
  • 6-8 bok choy leaves, chopped small
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 Tbsp. Sesame oil
  • 3 Tbsp. Soy Sauce
  • 100-150 won-ton or dumpling wrappers (I like won-ton best)
  • 1 teaspoon hot oil
  • 1/3 c. soy sauce
  • 1/3 c. vinigar
  • 1 T. sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon hot oil
  • Pot Sticker Dipping Sauce

METHOD

  1. Mix filling ingredients thoroughly, best if you use your hands.
  2. Loosely wrap stack of wrappers in a slightly damp dishtowel, this will keep them from drying out as you work. Have a bowl of warm water and a tray for the finished dumplings handy.
  3. Put a wrapper in your open hand. Put a small amount of filling in the middle of the wrapper. Use the warm water to moisten 1/2 of the edge and fold. Pinch/fold together.
  4. Cooking: Lightly coat a frying pan with oil (best is peanut oil). Fry dumplings in med-high heat until brown (3-5 min.) then turn. Fry second side until just brown, then add a tablespoon of water to pan, cover and finish cooking until most of the water is gone.
  5. Serve with dipping sauce. SOURCE: Kathy Herring
